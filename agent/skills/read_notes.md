# Reading Notes

When the user asks about their notes, follow this procedure:

## Step 1 — Fetch

Call `list_notes` to retrieve their notes. If they named a topic or tag (e.g. "my notes on goals"), pass that as the `tag` filter. Otherwise fetch the default set.

## Step 2 — Read, don't just list

Don't recite the notes back verbatim like a data dump. Read them the way a thoughtful guide would — looking for:

- **Themes**: What topics keep coming up? What does the user clearly care about?
- **Patterns**: Are there recurring feelings, tensions, or aspirations?
- **Connections to their dreams**: Does anything link to bigger goals they've mentioned?
- **Surprises**: Anything unexpected or worth calling out?

## Step 3 — Reflect back

Give a brief, grounded readout. Lead with the most meaningful observation, not the most recent note. You might say something like:

> "You've captured 8 notes. A few themes stand out — relationships and giving keep coming up, alongside some philosophy about what winning really means. Want to explore any of these?"

Keep it short. The user can ask to go deeper on anything.

## Step 4 — Offer to go further

If patterns suggest something worth acting on, name it. If a note connects to a goal they've mentioned, surface that link. Don't force it — but don't miss it either.
